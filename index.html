Mission statement

In many parts of the world ecosystems are degraded and under extreme pressure from climate change, biodiversity loss, population increase, hunger and mismanagement of resources. Human, animal and plant inhabitants of planet Earth suffer because of this.

ESMERALDA, which is an acronym for Earth Sustainability Monitoring for Ecosystem Restoration using sAteLlites to foster Development for humAnkind, is an effort by four private companies from France and Germany with different expertise to leverage financial and space technology know-how to conceptualize ecosystem restoration projects with a focus on sustainable business models for the population of the areas to be restored.

The ESMERALDA approach rests on four pillars:

  1. Identification of areas and ecosystems to restore, involving all relevant stakeholders at local, regional and global levels, moderating a project concept and devising a sustainable business plan
  2. Matching the projects with funding opportunities
  3. Providing transparency and traceability of the project implementation over the complete decades long life-cycle using satellite data and geographical information systems (GIS).
  4. Sharing of knowledge and educating populations living in areas to be restored.

To scale up ecosystem restoration activities in Africa and beyond the ESMERALDA seed fund, in the process of being established in Luxembourg and thus subject to European banking regulations, will accept your investments. Contact us today at info@esmeralda.earth to discuss your specific objectives

About

The ESMERALDA consortium consists of:

Everimpact

a French company dedicated to measuring real carbon emissions to finance impactful climate action.

VisioTerra

a French company specialising in scientific consulting for Earth observation.

Climate & Company

a German think-tank specialized in sustainable finance.

mundialis

a German company using Free and Open Source Software for Earth observation data analysis, spatial data infrastructures and high performance computing.
